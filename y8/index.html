Year 8 Maths Scheme of Work

Select a topic on the left

Resources

Fractions, Decimals, Percentages

  • Converting between fractions, decimals, and percentages including recurring decimals
  • Understanding of 0.9 recurring

Percentage Change

  • State a value as a percentage of another value.
  • Increase or decrease an amount by a given percentage. Use of scale factors.
  • Calculate a percentage change
  • Find the original value of something from its value after a percentage change
  • Carry out compound interest problems

Rounding

  • Round values to a given number of decimal places or significant figures
  • Estimate values by rounding to 1sf

Algebra

  • Substitute values into algebraic expressions
  • Expand linear brackets
  • Solve equations with one variable and linear brackets on both sides
  • Solving equations involving fractions
  • Changing the subject of an equation

Averages

  • Review Mean, Median, Mode, Range
  • Discrete Frequency Tables

Indices

  • Multiplication and division rules
  • Positive and negative integers only

Standard Form

  • Difference between decimal and standard form
  • Change numbers from decimal form to standard form
  • Add subtract multiply divide standard form, leaving answers in standard form (with and without calculators)

Pythagoras' Theorem

  • Understand and use the word "hypotenuse"
  • Use Pythagoras' Theorem to find any missing lengths
  • Multi step problems

Similarity

  • Using a Scale Factor to find lengths of similar shapes
  • Harder examples, including finding similar triangles in parallel lines, bowtie shapes, and nested triangles
  • Using algebra to find lengths

Plotting Graphs

  • Understand that a graph is a set of points that satisfy a particular equation
  • Be able to make a table of values for a given equation and then plotting this line on a set of axes
  • Recognise the general shape (and names) of Linear, Quadratic, Cubic and Circular graphs

Sets

  • Understand and use set notation
  • Be able to list the elements of a set, know that sets use curly brackets, E, union, intersection, complement, n(A), empty set, exists/does not exist, is part of the set/is not part of the set
  • Be able to draw and shade a Venn diagram

Tree Diagrams

  • Use of tree diagrams in compound proportion questions

Probability

  • Independent Events
  • Tree diagrams

Perimeter, Area and Volume

  • Revision of triangles, parallelograms & trapeziums
  • Area and circumference of circles, including leaving answers as decimals or multiples of pi
  • Volumes of prisms, including cylinders

Simultaneous Equations

  • Both elimination and substitution methods
  • Be able to form and solve equations from worded problem
  • Understand that the crossing points of two lines are the solutions to the equations

Circle Theorems

  • Identify and label the following: circumference, radius, diameter, arc, sector, segment, chord
  • 4 basic circle theorems

Circle Theorems

  • Identify and label the following: circumference, radius, diameter, arc, sector, segment, chord
  • 4 basic circle theorems

Rearranging Formulae

  • Be able to change the subject of formulae, including equations with fractions, brackets, roots and powers.
  • Be able to substitute values into formulae

Parallel Lines

  • Angles relating to parallel lines

Bearings

  • 3 rules of bearings

Algebra

  • Be able to expand double brackets

Trigonometry

  • Understand the tan ratio

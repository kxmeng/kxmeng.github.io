Simultaneous Equations working
Example 1 - question 6:
David buys 2 DVDs and 2 CDs in a shop and they cost £18
Ellie buys 3 DVDs and 2 CDs in the same shop and they cost £22
Let \(c\) be the price of a CD and \(d\) be the price of a DVD.
\( \begin{align*} \color{blue}\boxed{1}&: 2c + 2d = 18\\ \color{blue}\boxed{2}&: 2c + 3d = 22\\ \color{blue}\boxed{2}-\boxed{1}&: d = 4\\ \color{blue}\mathrm{sub\ into\ }\boxed{1}&: 2c + 8 = 18 \Rightarrow c = 5 \end{align*}\)
Important points:
Labelling your constants (if necessary). It's "obvious" that \(c\) might mean CD rather than DVD, but it's a good habit to get into for when things become less obvious.
Labelling your equations. This makes it easy to refer to them (I normally use a circle instead of a square because it's easier to draw, but the important thing is not to just write the number because that is confusing), and it's really easy to see how I got line 3 with these labels.
Example 2 - question 8:
\( \begin{align*} \color{blue}\boxed{1}&: 3x+2y=16\\ \color{blue}\boxed{2}&: 2x-3y=2\\ \color{blue}\boxed{3} = 2\times\boxed{1}&: 6x+4y=32\\ \color{blue}\boxed{4} = 3\times\boxed{2}&: 6x-9y=6\\ \color{blue}\boxed{3}-\boxed{4}&: 13y=26 \Rightarrow y=2\\ \color{blue}\mathrm{sub\ into\ }\boxed{1}&: 3x + 4 = 16 \Rightarrow x = 4 \end{align*}\)

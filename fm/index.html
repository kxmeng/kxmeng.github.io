A Level Further Maths Scheme of Work

NB - this is not currently up to date!

Resources

Binomial Theorem

  • Positive integer powers only

Indices, Surds

  • Laws of indices, including solving equations e.g. \(25^x = 125^{2-x}\)
  • Arithmetic of surds, including rationalising denominators and using the binomial theorem
  • Find the square root of expressions e.g. \(7 + 4\sqrt{3}\)

Matrices 1

  • Basic definitions and operations
  • Transformations using matrices
  • Determinant of a 2 by 2 matrix and its interpretation as an area scale factor. Effect of positive/negative determinant on orientation
  • \(\mathrm{det}(\mathrm{\textbf{AB}}) = \mathrm{det}(\mathrm{\textbf{A}})\mathrm{det}(\mathrm{\textbf{B}})\)
  • Invariant points and lines
  • Describing geometrically the transformation associated with a given matrix
  • Given a transformation, find its matrix
  • Understand the meaning of conformable, equal, square, rectangular, m by n, zero and null, transpose and identity when applied to matrices
  • Definitions of associative and commutative

Polynomial Division

  • Different methods for polynomial division
  • Remainder Theorem
  • Factor Theorem for factors \((x - a)\) and \((bx - a)\), and harder ones e.g. \(a^3 + b^3\), \((a - b)^3 + (b - c)^3 + (c - a)^3\)

Sums of Series

  • Arithmetic and Geometric progressions and their sums
  • Method of differences
  • Sigma notation
  • Using standard formulae for \(\displaystyle\sum r^n\) for \(n=1,2,3\)

Simultaneous Equations

  • Systems of 3 or more equations
  • Gaussian elimination
  • Consistent, inconsistent and ambiguous sets of equations
  • Solution or failure of solution of three simultaneous linear equations in terms of the geometrical arrangement of three planes

Matrices 2

  • Inverse of a matrix (2 by 2 and 3 by 3)
  • \((\mathrm{\textbf{AB}})^{-1} = \mathrm{\textbf{B}}^{-1}\mathrm{\textbf{A}}^{-1}\)
  • Inverse transformations
  • Significance of zero determinant, singular and non-singular matrices
  • Solving simultaneous equations using inverse matrices
  • Transformations in 3 dimensions, and determinant as volume scale factor

Exponentials and Logarithms

  • Logarithms – definitions, relation to index laws, change of base, solving equations (no natural logarithms at this point)
  • Exponential functions and their graphs
  • Problems involving exponential growth and decay
  • Modelling problems with sequences and series. e.g. problems involving compound and simple interest on bank deposits, loans, mortgages, etc. and other contexts in which growth or decay can be modelled by an arithmetic or geometric sequence
  • Be able to use logarithmic graphs to estimate parameters in relationships of the form \(y = ax^n\) and \(y = kb^x\), given data, both by drawing graphs and using calculator functions

Trigonometry

  • Radian measure – equations, arc length and sector area
  • Sine and cosine rules, including the ambiguous case, and area of triangle
  • Know and be able to use exact values for \(0\), \(\frac{\pi}{6}\), \(\frac{\pi}{4}\), \(\frac{\pi}{3}\), \(\pi\) etc

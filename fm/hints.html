Hints

  • Paper A (20 marks)
    1. Let \(y = \mathrm{arcsin}\ x\), then find \(\dfrac{\mathrm{d}y}{\mathrm{d}x}\)
    2. Integrate by parts
  • Paper B (20 marks)
    1. To differentiate \(\mathrm{arccos} x\), let \(y = \mathrm{arccos} x\) and find \(\dfrac{\mathrm{d}y}{\mathrm{d}x}\)
    2. Integrate by parts (write as \(1\times ...\))
  • Paper C
  • Paper D (25 marks)
    1. a) formula booklet; b) split into two fractions; c) shouldn't need a hint for this one
    2. Integrate by parts, remembering that \(\sec^2 x\) is really easy to integrate
  • Paper E (9 marks)
    1. Fairly standard, though difficult, substitution, shouldn't need a hint for this
  • Paper F (20 marks)
    1. Integrate by parts, using the same method as for \(\displaystyle\int\ln x\ \mathrm{d}x\)
    2. Standard, though difficult, partial fractions question
  • Paper G (29 marks)
    1. Chain rule. To differentiate \(\mathrm{arctan} x\), let \(y = \mathrm{arctan} x\) and find \(\dfrac{\mathrm{d}y}{\mathrm{d}x}\)
    2. Let \(t = \tan x\) - realistically wont be required to figure out this substitution
    3. Integrate by parts, remembering that integrating \(\sin^nx\) is very difficult for \(n > 1\)

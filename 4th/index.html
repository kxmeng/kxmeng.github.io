Indices

  1. use index notation and index laws for multiplication and division of positive and negative integer powers including zero
  2. use index laws to simplify and evaluate numerical expressions involving integer, fractional and negative powers

Standard Form

  1. calculate with and interpret numbers in standard form
  2. solve problems involving standard form

Pythagoras' Theorem

  1. know, understand and use Pythagoras' theorem in two and three dimensions

Trigonometry

  1. know, understand and use sine, cosine and tangent of acute angles to determine lengths and angles of a right angled triangle
  2. understand and use angles of elevation and depression

Linear equations

  1. solve linear equations, with integer or fractional coefficients, in one unknown
  2. find the equation of a straight line parallel or perpendicular to a given line

Linear graphs

  1. find the gradient of a straight line
  2. recognise that equations of the form \(y = mx + c\) are straight line graphs with gradient \(m\) and intercept on the y-axis \((0,c)\)

Changing the subject

  1. understand the process of manipulating formulae or equations to change the subject, to include cases where the subject may appear twice or a power of the subject occurs

Simultaneous equations

  1. calculate the exact solution of two simultaneous equations in two unknowns
  2. interpret the equations as lines and the common solution as the point of intersection

Multiplying out brackets

  1. expand the product of two simple linear expressions

Factorising quadratics

  1. understand the concept of a quadratic expression and be able to factorise such expressions

Solving quadratics

  1. solve quadratic equations by factorisation
  2. form and solve quadratic equations from data given in a context

Percentages, proportional change

  1. solve simple percentage problems, including percentage increase and decrease
  2. use reverse percentages
  3. use compound interest and depreciation
  4. use repeated percentage change
  5. solve compound interest problems

Area and volume

  1. understand and use the properties of the parallelogram, rectangle, square, rhombus, trapezium and kite
  2. find the area of simple shapes using the formulae for the areas of triangles and rectangles
  3. find the area of parallelograms and trapezia
  4. recognise and give names of: cube, cuboid, prism, pyramid, cylinder, sphere, cone
  5. find the surface area of simple shapes using the area formulae for triangles and rectangles
  6. find the surface area of a cylinder
  7. find the volume of prisms, including cuboids and cylinders, using an appropriate formula
  8. convert between units of volume within the metric system
  9. find the surface area and volume of a sphere and a right circular cone using relevant formulae

Arc, sector, segment

  1. recognise the terms 'centre', 'radius', 'chord', 'diameter', 'circumference', 'tangent', 'arc', 'sector' and 'segment' of a circle
  2. find circumferences and areas of circles; find perimeters and areas of sectors of circles

Average, range

  1. understand the concept of average
  2. calculate the mean, median, mode and range for a discrete data set
  3. calculate an estimate for the mean for grouped data
  4. identify the modal class for grouped data
  5. find the interquartile range from a discrete set of data

Cumulative frequency, box plots

  1. construct cumulative frequency diagrams from tabulated data
  2. use cumulative frequency diagrams
  3. estimate the median from a cumulative frequency diagram
  4. estimate the interquartile range from a cumulative frequency diagram

Histograms

  1. construct and interpret histograms

Bounds

  1. identify upper and lower bounds where values are given to a degree of accuracy
  2. solve problems using upper and lower bounds where values are given to a degree of accuracy

Similarity, congruence

  1. understand congruence as meaning the same shape and size
  2. understand that two or more polygons with the same shape and size are said to be congruent to each other
  3. understand and use the geometrical properties that similar figures have corresponding lengths in the same ratio but corresponding angles remain unchanged

Area volume of similar shapes

  1. understand that areas of similar figures are in the ratio of the square of corresponding sides
  2. understand that volumes of similar figures are in the ratio of the cube of corresponding sides

Inequalities

  1. understand and use the symbols \(<, >, \leq, \geq\)
  2. understand and use the convention for open and closed intervals on a number line
  3. solve simple linear inequalities in one variable and represent the solution set on a number line
  4. represent simple linear inequalities on rectangular Cartesian graphs

Shading regions

  1. identify regions on rectangular Cartesian graphs defined by simple linear inequalities

Transformations

  1. understand that rotations are specified by a centre and an angle
  2. rotate a shape about a point through a given angle
  3. recognise that an anti-clockwise rotation is a positive angle of rotation and a clockwise rotation is a negative angle of rotation
  4. understand that reflections are specified by a mirror line
  5. construct a mirror line given an object and reflect a shape given a mirror line
  6. understand that translations are specified by a distance and a direction
  7. translate a shape
  8. understand and use column vectors in translations
  9. understand that rotations, reflections and translations preserve length and angle so that a transformed shape under any of these transformations remains congruent to the original shape
  10. understand that enlargements are specified by a centre and a scale factor
  11. understand that enlargements preserve angles and not lengths
  12. enlarge a shape given the scale factor
  13. identify and give complete descriptions of transformations
Worksheets: Translations, Describing translations, Reflections, Describing reflections, Rotations, Enlargements, Centre of enlargement, Describing enlargements, Negative scale factor enlargements
Corbett Maths: Translations (4:47), Reflections (3:40), Describing reflections (5:37), Rotations (5:24), Enlargements (2:30), Describing enlargements (6:31), Finding centre of enlargement (4:55), Fractional scale factor (3:31), Negative scale factor (5:11)

Probability

  1. understand the language of probability: outcomes, equal likelihood, events, random
  2. understand and use the probability scale
  3. understand and use estimates or measures of probability from theoretical models
  4. find probabilities from a Venn diagram
  5. understand the concepts of a sample space and an event, and how the probability of an event can be determined from the sample space
  6. list all the outcomes for single events and for two successive events in a systematic way
  7. estimate probabilities from previously collected data
  8. calculate the probability of the complement of an event happening
  9. use the addition rule of probability for mutually exclusive events
  10. understand and use the term 'expected frequency'
  11. draw and use tree diagrams
  12. determine the probability that two or more independent events will occur
  13. use simple conditional probability when combining events
Worksheets: Sample space, Relative frequency, Not happening, mutually exclusive, Independent events, Tree diagrams
Corbett Maths: Sample space (4:14), Relative frequency (7:54), Not happening (4:08), Independent events (6:46), Mutually exclusive (6:37), Tree diagrams (7:45)

Speed, distance, time and curved graphs

  1. understand and use the relationship between average speed, distance and time
  2. find the gradients of non-linear graphs by drawing a tangent
Worksheets: Speed, distance, time
Corbett Maths: Speed, distance, time (13:51)
Other: Tangent to curved graphs (5:41)

Plotting quadratics

  1. recognise, generate points and plot graphs of linear and quadratic functions

Transformations of quadratics

  1. apply simple transformations to quadratic graphs

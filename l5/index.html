Surds and indices

  1. use index notation and index laws for multiplication and division of positive and negative integer powers including zero
  2. understand the meaning of surds
  3. manipulate surds, including rationalising a denominator
  4. use index laws to simplify and evaluate numerical expressions involving integer, fractional and negative powers

Trigonometry

  1. know, understand and use Pythagoras' theorem in two and three dimensions
  2. know, understand and use sine, cosine and tangent of acute angles to determine lengths and angles of a right angled triangle
  3. understand and use sine, cosine and tangent of obtuse angles
  4. understand and use angles of elevation and depression
  5. apply trigonometrical methods to solve problems in three dimensions, including finding the angle between a line and a plane

Circle theorems

  1. understand chord and tangent properties of circles
    2. recognise the term 'cyclic quadrilateral' (and its properties)
  2. understand and use angle properties of the circle: angle subtended by an arc at the centre is twice the angle at the circumference; angle subtended at the circumference by a diameter is a right angle; angles in the same segment are equal; opposite angles in a cyclic quadrilateral sum to 180 degrees; the alternate segment theorem

Sets

  1. understand the definition of a set
  2. use the set notation \(\cup,\ \cap,\ \in,\ \notin\)
  3. understand the concept of the universal set and the empty set and the symbols for these sets
  4. understand and use the complement of a set
  5. use Venn diagrams to represent sets
  6. understand sets defined in algebraic terms, and understand and use subsets
  7. use Venn diagrams to represent sets and the number of elements in sets
  8. use the notation n(A) for the number of elements in the set A
  9. use sets in practical situations

  4. recognise, plot and draw cubic graphs where the letters \(x\) and \(y\) can be replaced with any two other letters

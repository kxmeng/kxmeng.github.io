Coordinate geometry

    1. Solve algebraically by writing the equation of the lines in the form \(y = ...\), then equating the two equations since at the point of intersection, the \(y\) values of both lines must be the same. Once the \(x\) coordinate is found, put it into the equation of either line.
    1. \(x+5=2x \Rightarrow x=5, y = 10\)
    2. \(2x-5=x+5 \Rightarrow x=10, y = 15\)
    3. Rearrange left equation \(y = 5-x\); then \(x+5=2x \Rightarrow x=5, y = 0\)
    4. Left equation: \(y = 7-2x\); right equation: \(y = \dfrac{x-6}{2}\); then \(7-2x = \dfrac{x-6}{2} \Rightarrow x=4, y = -1\)
    5. Left equation: \(y = \dfrac{1-4x}{3}\); then \(\dfrac{1-4x}{3} = 1-x \Rightarrow x=-2, y = 3\)

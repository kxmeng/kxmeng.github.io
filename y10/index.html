Year 10 Maths Scheme of Work

Select a topic on the left

Resources

Algebra

  • Brief review of basic concepts: Variables, unknowns, expressions, identities, equations, substituting values
  • Expanding single, double and triple brackets e.g. \((x^2+3x+1)(5x+7)\), \((a-b)(2b+c)(c-3a)\)
  • e.g. \((x^2 + 3x - 4y)(5x + 2y + 7)\)
  • Basic application of Binomial theorem using Pascal's triangle only, up to expanding e.g. \((2x-3y^2)^5\)
  • Addition and multiplication of polynomials
  • Use of calculator to find Binomial coefficients
  • Use Binomial theorem for truncated expansions, finding specific terms, using \(x=10^n\) to approximate values
  • Apply Binomial Theorem to e.g. trinomial expansions

Percentages

  • Finding a percentage of an amount
  • Expressing one amount as a percentage of another
  • Percentage increase/decrease
  • Reverse percentages
  • Simple and compound interest problems

Right Angled Triangles

  • Pythagoras and SOHCAHTOA
  • 3D problems
  • Volumes of pyramids (including cones)
  • Include bearings (and other angle rules where possible)
  • \(\tan\theta \equiv \dfrac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}\) and \(\sin^2\theta+\cos^2\theta \equiv 1\)

Histograms (2 weeks)

  • Finding mean, median, mode, range, quartiles and interquartile range from raw and grouped data
  • Cumulative frequency graphs
  • Histograms

Indices

  • Index laws
  • Solving basic equations e.g. \(x^4 = 16\), \(2^{x+4} = 2^{6-x}\)
  • Include non-calculator evaluation e.g. \(\frac{272}{3}\), \(\frac{4}{9}-\frac{3}{2}\)
  • Include practice of moving between indices and surds
  • Solving equations which make use of index laws and substitutions e.g. \(3^x = 27^{2x + 3}\), \(8^{2x} = 4^{2-x}\)

Quadratics (1.5 weeks)

  • Factorising and completing the square including e.g. \(2a^2-2a-12\), including difference of two squares e.g. \(b^2-121\)
  • Completed square form to identify max and min values of expressions
  • More difficult examples e.g. \(2pq - 4p + 3q - 6\), \(18p^3q^2 - 27p^2q^2 + 30p^3q - 45p^2q\)
  • Include quadratic on denominator of a fraction
  • Include more examples, e.g. quadratic squared

Circle Theorems (1.5 weeks)

  • Angle in a semicircle, angles at circumference, angle at centre is double angle at circumference, cyclic quadrilaterals
  • Tangents and radii, alternate segment theorem, intersecting chords, including one or two tangents (see old IGCSE book 2), note isosceles triangles formed by radii
  • Completed square form to identify max and min values of expressions
  • Other interesting circle geometry problems, such as: finding the perpendicular bisector of a chord, which passes through the centre of the circle; finding the equations of radii and tangents etc

Surds (1.5 weeks)

  • Define and be able to identify rational and irrational numbers. Is the sum of two irrational numbers always irrational?
  • Arithmetic (including multiplication of multiple, multi-term surd expressions) & reduction
  • Rationalising denominators, including compound denominators such as \(2\sqrt{3} - 3\sqrt{5}\)
  • Binomial expansions which include surds e.g. \((3 - \sqrt{2})^4\), \((\sqrt{2} + 3\sqrt{7})^5\)
  • Harder surd denominators e.g. \(2 + \sqrt{3} + \sqrt{5}\)
  • Prove irrationality of \(\sqrt{2}\)

Standard Form

  • Converting between decimal and standard forms
  • Multiplying and dividing numbers in standard form, including algebraic expressions in which the correct standard form depends on the value of the variable(s) e.g. \(\dfrac{x^2\times10^2}{3x\times10^{-3}} = \dfrac{x}{3}\times 10^5\ \mathrm{or}\ \dfrac{10x}{3}\times10^4\)
  • Converting between fractions and recurring decimals

Algebra

  • Change the subject of a formula
  • Algebraic proof
  • Complete square in order to change the subject of a quadratic
  • Include consideration of taking the positive and/or negative square root, depending on context
  • Divisibility proofs

Mensuration

  • Arc length and sector area
  • Recaps volume of prisms, pyramids and cones
  • Surface area of cones, and volume and surface area of spheres, hemispheres, and compound solids

Quadratics (2 weeks)

  • Solve quadratic equations by factorising and completing the square
  • Solve higher order equations e.g. \((2x + 1)(x - 3)(x + 5) = 0\)
  • Derive and use quadratic formula
  • All students need to be able to sketch a quadratic graph with all intercepts and vertex labelled
  • Use completed square form to justify whether or not the graph crosses the x-axis.
  • Hidden quadratics e.g. \(x^6 - x^3 - 20 = 0\), \(t^{\frac{2}{3}}-6t^{\frac{1}{3}}+9=0\)
  • Brief look at the the discriminant and relationship with number of solutions of a quadratic
  • Some polynomial division by inspection (incl language of quotients and remainders)
  • Factor and remainder theorems (very simple applications only)
  • Finding integer solutions to e.g. \(xy - 2x + 3y - 12 = 0\)

Probability (1.5 weeks)

  • Review of Y8 probability, including mutual exclusivity and sample spaces
  • Independence, conditional probability and tree diagrams. Students should be able to recognise and interpret "given that" statements
  • More challenging conditional probability; "reversing" tree diagrams; Venn diagrams

Probability

  • Using scale factors to calculate corresponding lengths in similar shapes. Use of the square/cube of a linear scale factor to find areas/volumes of similar shapes and solids
  • Use as an opportunity to recap last half termâ€™s mensuration topic
  • Apply to volume and surface area of frustums of cones

Simultaneous Equations (0.5 weeks)

  • Substituting a linear expression into a quadratic expression

Simultaneous Equations (1.5 weeks)

  • Area of a triangle, sine rule (including ambiguous case), cosine rule
  • Include polygon rules e.g. find area of a triangle inside a pentagon
  • Thorough treatment of ambiguous case of sine rule - use constructions and symmetry AND the sine graph

Trigonometric Graphs (0.5 weeks)

  • Recognising and sketching sin, cos and tan graphs
  • Solving very basic trig equations e.g. \(\cos x = 0.2\) for a range of \(x\)
  • A few harder equations e.g. \(\sin 2x = 0.7\) and \(\sin (x + 30) = -0.5\), including a couple which use identities

Sets and Venn Diagrams (1.5 weeks)

  • Review Y8 set theory and introduce set builder notation

Graph Sketching (1.5 weeks)

  • Sketch quadratic graphs, labelling all intercepts and the vertex
  • Recognising and sketching graphs of general cubics, \(x^2+y^2=r^2\), reciprocal graphs
  • Use Pythagoras' Theorem to justify general equation of circle
  • Visualise and sketch graphs of higher degree polynomials
  • Find centre and radius of circle from expanded equation by completing square in each variable

Graphical Solutions (0.5 weeks)

  • Use graphs to solve equations
  • Using trig graphs to solve equations, relate to ambiguous case of sine rule
  • Start to solve equations with multiple steps, quadratics in trig functions and equations which require the use of trigonometric identities.

Direct and Inverse Proportion (1.5 weeks)

  • Include some examples of problems involving ratio and proportion
  • Graphs of quantities in direct or inverse proportion
  • Questions where one quantity is proportional/inversely proportional to the square, cube, square root, etc of another

Units (0.5 weeks)

  • Convert area and volume units; compound units density/pressure

Transformations of Graphs (1.5 weeks)

  • Translations and stretches of graphs, including trig graphs
  • Relationship between quadratics in completed square form and translations of \(y = x^2\)
  • Relate eqn of circle to translation of eqn of circle centred at origin
  • Reflecting graphs
  • More interesting transformations of trig graphs, including reflections and compound transformations

Arithmetic Sequences

  • Use linear expressions to describe the \(n\)th term of an arithmetic sequence
  • Understand and use common difference, \(d\), and first term, \(a\), in an arithmetic sequence
  • know and use \(n\)th term \(= a + (n - 1)d\)
  • Find the sum of the first \(n\) terms, \(S_n\) of an arithmetic series
  • Generalising the terms of other sequences (building on Y7 work), including plenty of figural sequences (incl non-linear) and some geometric sequences
  • Continued fractions, nested surds, series that contain themselves
